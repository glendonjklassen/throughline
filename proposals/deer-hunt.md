## Human Proposal

This one was generated by a human so it's extra special:

Create a new branch and it will represent a new scenario where we are going to stretch the engine a bit. There's been a bit too much touchy-feely on the engine so we're going to create a multiplayer scenario with hunters. They're all hunting in the same forest which has several different locations. There is an NPC deer which moves around from location to location. This is intended to be played EITHER together (e.g. syncing every move) or alone with sync later.

The key is there are several outcomes:
- a player may shoot the deer, in which case the deer is dead and no one else may hunt it (assuming sync happens)
- a player may shoot the deer WHEN THE DEER IS COLOCATED with hunters in which case there is a 90% chance of shooting the deer (which is a win, but the other hunter is mad at you), and a 10% chance of shooting the hunter (which is obviously a catastrophe)
- a player may shoot and scare away the deer for all players.
- a player may do any of these things and then sync up with another player, and there will be weirdness Schroeginger's-deer-style

We want the scenario and corresponding tests for all possible outcomes and a minimum of 3 players. There should also be several locations so this doesn't end trivially - there should be a bit of the thrill of the hunt. Use tension for console edginess to simulate buck fever when you can shoot at the deer. Let's also have "hunting ability" increase as the player plays more.

# Iterated AI Resultant Proposal

## Setting

A section of land in southern Manitoba, mid-November rifle season. One square mile bounded by grid roads on all four sides. Mix of harvested crop fields and bush — typical parkland habitat. Three hunters approach from different roads.

## Locations (~50)

The section is a square mile. The top-level zones are connected as before, but each zone is a **subgraph** of ~6 nodes with random-feeling interconnections. You can get turned around in the bush. That's the point.

### Top-level zones

**Roads (entry points, 1-2 nodes each):**
- North Road (truck, gear)
- South Road (truck, gear)
- West Road (truck, gear)

**Fields (3-4 nodes each):**
- North Field — harvested wheat stubble. Open. Can see a long way. Nodes: field edge, stubble rows, hay bale, drainage ditch
- South Field — harvested canola stubble. Nodes: field edge, stubble flat, fence line, slough edge

**Bush (6+ nodes each — this is where you get lost):**
- Bush Edge — scrubby poplar, patchy brush. Transition between field and real forest. Nodes: thin poplars, brush pile, game trail entrance, old fence, clearing, deadfall
- Oak Ridge — thick bur oak on a low ridge, primary bedding area. Dense. Nodes: ridge top, oak thicket, scrape line, mossy hollow, blowdown, deer trail
- Willow Bottom — low wet ground, willows, cattails. Nodes: cattail marsh, willow tangle, creek crossing, mud flat, beaver dam, dry hummock
- Poplar Stand — open poplar bush, travel corridor. Nodes: poplar alley, birch clump, rub line, open understory, game trail fork, windbreak

### Zone connections (top level)
```
North Road -- North Field -- Bush Edge -- Oak Ridge
                               |             |
West Road -- South Field -- Poplar Stand -- Willow Bottom
                |
            South Road
```

Within each zone, nodes are interconnected semi-randomly (not a clean grid — some dead ends, some loops). Moving between zones requires finding an edge node that connects to the next zone.

### Why this matters
You enter the bush from a field edge. You take a game trail. You hit a deadfall. You backtrack. You find the scrape line. You're not sure which direction camp is anymore. That's hunting.

## Characters

- **1+ players** — all human. Each starts at a different road. Solo works fine. Multiple players each run their own session and sync. No NPC hunters.
- **The Deer** — NPC. One whitetail buck.

Tests use 3 players minimum to cover co-location and sync.

## Time

Each tick = 5 minutes. Legal shooting: 7 AM to 7 PM = 144 ticks per day. Multi-day hunt — nightfall means you head back to your truck, come back tomorrow morning. The deer will be somewhere different. You might know the land better.

The hunt is slow. Most ticks you're walking, waiting, looking around, seeing nothing. That's the game.

## Movement

Two modes, and this is the core choice:

**Move fast** — cover two nodes per tick. You're walking with purpose, maybe pushing through brush. You cover ground but you're loud. Higher spook chance if the deer is nearby. Good for getting across the section to where you think the deer is. Bad if it's already close.

**Move slow** — one node per tick. You're picking your steps, stopping to look and listen. Lower spook chance. This is how you move when you know the deer is in the area. Also how you hunt if you're experienced enough to be patient.

**Sit and wait** — zero nodes. You're on a stump or leaning against a tree. Lowest spook chance possible. The deer might walk right past you. Or it might not come your way at all and you've burned daylight. This is how a lot of deer actually get taken.

The choice between covering ground and being quiet is the whole game. A new hunter thinks they need to find the deer. An experienced hunter knows the deer will find them.

## The Deer

Mid-November, rut is on. The buck is moving more during daylight than usual but still follows loose patterns:

| Time | General area | What it's doing |
|---|---|---|
| Early morning | Fields | Feeding on stubble |
| Mid-morning | Bush Edge | Heading for cover |
| Midday | Oak Ridge | Bedded down |
| Afternoon | Poplar Stand | Starting to move again |
| Evening | Fields | Feeding again |

But this is a *tendency*, not a schedule. The deer drifts between nodes within its current zone. Some days it beds in Willow Bottom instead of Oak Ridge. Some mornings it never leaves the bush. Rut bucks do weird things.

If spooked, the deer bolts to a different zone. You lost it. Go find it again. If you miss your shot, it's gone — off the section, `DeerGone`, hunt's over. That's why you don't shoot until you're sure.

Sometimes you walk out of your truck at 7:05 AM and there's a ten-point buck standing in the ditch 80 yards away. That can happen too.

## Spooking

Random. Experience lowers the odds but doesn't eliminate them.

When you enter a node where the deer is:
- The deer might not notice you (you're fine, take your shot when ready)
- The deer might spook and bolt to another zone (you blew it, but you can find it again)

Moving fast = higher spook chance. Moving slow = lower. Sitting still when the deer walks in = lowest. Experience shifts all of these in your favor.

No hard thresholds. A green hunter moving slow might still be fine. A veteran crashing through deadfall will still spook it. Randomness is the fundamental unit.

## Experience

You get better by hunting. Not by gaming stats.

- Time in the bush: you learn the land
- Finding sign: you start reading the terrain
- Each day: you slept on it, you know the section better

Experience improves your spook odds and your shot accuracy. It's a quiet background number, not something you optimize.

## Shooting

"Take the shot" — available when the deer is at your node.

Random outcome weighted by experience:
- **Clean kill**: You connected. `DeerKilled`. Done.
- **Miss**: `DeerGone`. It's off the property. Go home.
- **With another hunter present**: Same as above, but if you do connect there's a small chance (~10%) you hit the other hunter instead. `HunterShot`. The other hunter's Trust in you goes through the floor regardless of outcome — you shot in their direction.

Higher experience = higher chance of connecting. But even a good shot can miss, and even a bad one can get lucky.

## Tension

| Level | Trigger |
|---|---|
| 0 | At your truck |
| 2 | Walking through fields or bush, nothing happening |
| 4 | Found fresh sign — tracks, rub, scrape |
| 6 | You can see the deer (adjacent node or same zone, spotted) |
| 8 | Deer at your node — buck fever, hands shaking |
| 9 | Deer + another hunter at your node |
| 10 | Shot taken |

## Terminal Conditions

| Tag | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `DeerKilled` | Clean kill. Season's done. |
| `HunterShot` | Shot another hunter. Catastrophe. |
| `DeerGone` | Missed shot. It left the section. Go home empty. |

Note: spooking just relocates the deer. Only a missed shot ends the hunt permanently.

## Randomness

**Decision: Lamport-clock-seeded PRNG (option 1).**

Seed a `StdGen` from `lcTick (worldClock world)` combined with a per-use salt. This keeps axiom and action evaluation pure — no engine signature changes needed. `System.Random` is already a project dependency.

- **Deterministic** given the same world state — good for replay and sync convergence.
- **Unpredictable** to the player — the clock advances with each action, so different play sequences produce different outcomes.
- **Testable** — tests control the clock tick to produce known outcomes.
- **Extensible** — the seed can be augmented later (e.g. with world state hash or player-specific salt) without changing the interface.

Randomness lives entirely in scenario code (axiom evaluate functions and action definitions, both of which receive `GameWorld`). No engine changes required.

### Movement model

Fast/slow is a **pace toggle** (world tag `MovingFast`). Both modes move 1 node per action. Fast pace increases spook probability; slow pace decreases it. "Sit and wait" is a zero-movement action with the lowest spook chance. This avoids multi-step-per-tick engine changes while preserving the core choice: cover ground or stay quiet.

### Shot resolution

"Take the shot" is an action gated on `DeerSpotted` (set by axiom when player and deer are co-located and deer hasn't spooked). The action reads the clock, seeds a PRNG, factors in experience and co-located hunters, and returns the appropriate outcome effects. Pure and deterministic.

## Sync / Multiplayer Weirdness

When sessions merge:
- **Both killed the deer, same spot**: Not weird. It just took two bullets. Both get the kill.
- **Both killed the deer, different spots or different times**: That's the weirdness. One hunter killed it in North Field at 8 AM, another killed it in Oak Ridge at 2 PM. Schrodinger's Deer. Both need test cases.
- **One killed, one spooked**: Kill wins (ORSet). Spooker hears the shot from across the section.
- **One shot a hunter the other doesn't know about**: Existence conflict.

## Test Paths

1. Patient multi-day: scout day 1, find deer day 2, clean kill → `DeerKilled`
2. Stumble into deer early, spook it, relocate, find again, kill → `DeerKilled`
3. Miss the shot → `DeerGone`
4. Co-located with another hunter, kill deer → `DeerKilled` + Trust drop
5. Co-located with another hunter, hit the hunter → `HunterShot`
6. Nightfall → back to truck → new day → deer in different spot
7. Validation: no duplicate ActionIds, connected scene graph
